Gaussian profile

The GaussLaser type contains the parameters required for describing a Gaussian laser pulse. The spatial profile of the electric field is given by

\[\newcommand{\ii}{\mathrm{i}} % imaginary unit \begin{aligned} E_x (z,r) &= \xi_x E_0 \frac{w_0}{w} \exp\left(-\frac{r^2}{w^2} -\ii k z -\ii \frac{k r^2}{2 R} + \ii \phi_0\right)\\ E_y (z,r) &= \xi_y E_0 \frac{w_0}{w} \exp\left(-\frac{r^2}{w^2} -\ii k z -\ii \frac{k r^2}{2 R} + \ii \phi_0\right)\\ E_z (z,r) &= \frac{2 \left(\ii -\frac{z}{z_0}\right)}{kw^2 (z)} \left[xE_x (r,z) +yE_y(r,z)\right]\,, \end{aligned}\]

where

  • $E_0$ is the amplitude of the electric field
  • $\xi_x$ and $\xi_y$ give the polarization (choosing $\xi_x=1$ and $\xi_y=0$ for example, would give a linearly polarized field along the $x$ axis while taking both $1/\sqrt{2}$ would give circular polarization)
  • $w = w(z)$ is the beam radius at $z$
  • $w_0$ is the beam radius at the Rayleigh range $z=z_R$
  • $k$ is the wavenumber
  • $R$ is the radius of curvature
  • $\phi_0$ is the Gouy phase
